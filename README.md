## Entry Points for Contributors

**Start anywhere. Fork freely. No permission needed.**

### Story Entry Points

- Write a dialogue between Lucius and another exiled intellectual he meets on his journey, debating whether ideas can have a homeland.
- Describe a scene where Demetrius, the pragmatic scribe, must defend the 'useless' philosophy he records to a practical-minded local.
- Create a letter sent to Lucius from a former student still in Rome, grappling with loyalty to his teacher and fear of the regime.
- Imagine the final chapter from the perspective of the ox-cart driver, who has listened to every conversation and formed his own philosophy of the road.

### Technical Entry Points

- Build an interactive map tracing Lucius's journey from Rome, with geotagged excerpts of his dialogues and historical context for each location.
- Create a text analysis tool that compares the linguistic and rhetorical style of Lucius's 'exile dialogues' with his earlier, pre-exile writings (implied in the story).
